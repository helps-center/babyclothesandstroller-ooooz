1. Baby Clothes Basics

What to grab:

Onesies: Great for every day; easy diaper changes.

Sleepers/Rompers: One-piece outfits for sleep or playtime.

Footed Pajamas: Keep baby warm while they sleep; no socks needed.

T-shirts &amp; Pants: Good for layering.

Hats: Beanies for winter, sun hats for summer.

Socks &amp; Booties: Keep those little feet warm and prevent slips.

Swaddles/Sleep Sacks: Helps baby sleep safe and sound.

Jackets/Sweaters: For extra warmth when it's cold.

Mittens: Stop scratching and keep hands toasty.

2. Baby Clothes Sizes (Roughly)

Size: What Age? Notes

Newborn: 0-2 weeks: They grow out of these super quick!

0-3 months: Newborn - 3 months: You'll use lots of these early on.

3-6 months: 3-6 months: Might fit better than newborn size.

6-9 months: 6-9 months: When they start moving around more.

9-12 months: 9-12 months: Clothes that are easy to take off are a good idea.

12-18 months: 1 year+: Growth slows down a bit.

18-24 months: 1.5 - 2 years: Getting into toddler sizes now.

FYI: When in doubt, get the bigger size! Babies grow FAST.

3. What to Wear When?

Summer: Light cotton, short sleeves – anything breathable.

Winter: Layers are important! Think fleece or wool for outerwear, plus hats and mittens.

Rainy Season: Clothes that dry fast, waterproof jackets, and boots for toddlers.

4. What Fabrics Are Best?

Fabric: What's it like?

Cotton: Soft, lets skin breathe, and doesn't cause allergies. Great for new babies in General .

Organic Cotton: No yucky chemicals. Good for sensitive skin.

Bamboo Rayon: Really soft, keeps moisture away, and fights germs.

Fleece: Warm for cold days.

Wool: Naturally warm but needs special washing.

5. Some Safety Tips

No strings or anything they could choke on.

Flame-resistant pajamas (or snug-fitting ones).

No tight elastic or scratchy tags.

Double-check seams, buttons, and zippers.

6. Laundry

Wash baby clothes by themselves with gentle soap.

Don't use fabric softener or bleach.

Wash everything new before they wear it.

🛒 Baby Strollers: The Lowdown

1. Types of Strollers

Type: What's it for?

Full-Size Stroller: Tough, good for everything, usually has storage and seats that lean back.

Travel System: Comes with a car seat that clicks right into the stroller.

Lightweight/Umbrella: Small and easy to carry around.

Jogging Stroller: For running, with special tires and good suspension.

Double Stroller: For two kids. Can be side-by-side or one in front of the other.

Convertible Stroller: Grows with your kid.

Car Seat Carrier: Just a frame for the car seat.

2. What to Look For

Feature: Why it matters

Reclining Seat: So baby can sleep or sit up.

Harness: A 5-point harness is safest means secures shoulders, waist.

Wheel Type: Affects how smooth the ride is.

Suspension: Makes bumpy rides better.

Handle Height: For your comfort.

Brakes: For safety.

Folding: Easy to fold for trips or putting away.

Storage: For bags and toys.

Canopy: Keeps sun and rain away.

3. Safety First

Always use the harness!

Lock the brakes when you stop.

Don't hang heavy stuff on the handles.

Check for recalls.

Make sure there aren't sharp edges

4. What Stroller When?

Age: What's best?

Newborn: Travel system or a stroller that reclines all the way back.

3-6 months: Needs neck and head support.

6+ months: Most strollers work.

Toddlers: Needs a strong stroller with enough room.

Heads up: Not all strollers are good for newborns. Look for ones that recline flat or work with car seats.

5. Keeping it Clean

Wash the fabric with gentle soap now and then.

Oil the wheels if they squeak.

Keep it in a dry place.

Check the buckles

🧩 Extras

Stuff for Clothes: Bibs, burp cloths, etc.

Stuff for Strollers: Rain cover, bug net, cup holder, etc.

Boxes Buying Tips

Think ahead: Don't buy too much newborn stuff.

Read the fine print: Check the return rules, especially for strollers.

Make sure they’re comfy: For you and your baby.

Used stuff: Clothes are usually OK; be careful with used strollers (check safety requirements.)
**7. Dressing Your Baby: The Layering Way**

| Layer | What to Use | Why You Need It |
|---|---|---|
| Base | Onesies, bodysuits | First layer on the skin; helps keep baby's temp steady. |
| Mid | Pajamas, sleepers | Keeps baby warm; easy to take off if it gets too warm inside. |
| Outer | Jackets, snowsuits | Protects from cold, windy, or wet weather. |
| Extras | Hats, mittens, socks, blankets | For extra warmth and protection; add or remove as needed. |

**💡 Quick Tip:** Dress your baby in one more layer than you're wearing.

**8. Types of Baby Clothes**

*   **Preemie:** Made for tiny babies (under 5.5 lbs); super soft and small.
*   **Organic &amp; Hypoallergenic:** Best for babies with sensitive skin or eczema.
*   **Adaptive:** Designed for babies with special needs; easy to get on/off for medical reasons.
*   **Milestone:** Cute outfits for monthly pics or special days like birthdays.
*   **Holiday/Special Occasion:** Fancy clothes for parties or cultural events.

**9. Watch Out For These Clothing Problems**

*   Loose threads/buttons: Babies can choke on them.
*   Drawstrings: Can be a strangling danger.
*   Too-warm fabrics: Like fleece in summer, or too many layers, which can cause overheating.
*   Tight socks: Can stop blood flow.

**10. Dressing for the Weather**

*   **🔥 Hot &amp; Humid:**
    *   Use muslin or thin cotton.
    *   Rompers without sleeves, no socks inside.
    *   Light, breathable hats.
*   **❄️ Cold &amp; Snowy:**
    *   Wool or fleece for outerwear.
    *   Bodysuits plus footed sleepers work well together.
    *   Don't let baby get too hot indoors - check for sweating.
*   **☔ Rainy &amp; Wet:**
    *   Fabrics that dry fast.
    *   Waterproof outer layers, and always have extra clothes on hand.
    *   Dry baby quickly and change into dry clothes ASAP.

**11. Keep it Simple and Green**

*   **Capsule wardrobe:** Have a few clothes that all match, in colors that can be used for any baby.
*   **Hand-me-downs:** Great since babies grow fast.
*   **Natural dyes &amp; fabrics:** Better for baby's skin and the Earth.
*   **Buy used:** Often clothes are almost new; just wash them well first.

**🛞 BABY Buggies: The Details**

**6. Where Can You Go?**

| Where You're Going | What You Need |
|---|---|
| City Streets | Light, small, easy to turn |
| Gravel or Trails | Big wheels, air-filled tires, suspension |
| Snowy Roads | Wheels for all terrains, cozy footmuff |
| Beach/Sand | Wide tires, wide base |

**7. How They Fold Up**

| How It Folds | What It's Like |
|---|---|
| One-hand | Fast and easy, good when holding a baby |
| Stands on Its Own | Stays up when folded, good for storage |
| Folds Small | Fits in car trunks or on planes |
| Folds Flat | Saves space, but takes two hands |

**8. Handles**

| Handle Type | Why It's Good |
|---|---|
| Adjustable | Works for different sized people |
| Two-Way | Baby can face you or look out |
| Foam | Easy to hold but wears out over time |
| Leather-like | Lasts longer, looks nice, easy to wipe clean |

**9. Keeping it Clean**

| Part | How to Clean It |
|---|---|
| Fabric | Take it off and wash it if you can. Otherwise, wipe it down. |
| Frame | Wipe with a damp cloth and soap. Let it dry. |
| Wheels | Remove dirt and sand; put oil on if they squeak. |
| Straps | Use a brush and soapy water. |

**⚠️ Clean often, especially if someone else uses it or it's stored outside.**

**10. Taking Buggies with You**

| How You're Traveling | What to Do |
|---|---|
| Airplane | Use a small buggy or check it at the gate. |
| Car | Use a car seat that clicks into the buggy. Always buckle up! |
| Public Transport | Use light buggies that are skinny and have good brakes. |

**11. Extras**

| What It Is | What It Does |
|---|---|
| Snack tray | Holds snacks, drinks, toys |
| Stroller board | For an older kid to stand on |
| Footmuff | Keeps baby warm without blankets |
| Cup holder | For parents |
| Bug net | Keeps mosquitoes away |
| Sun shade | Protects from the sun |
| Rain cover | Keeps baby dry |

**12. When to Stop**

Most buggies are for kids up to 45-55 lbs or 4-5 years old.

Stop if:

*   Your kid doesn't want to sit in it anymore.
*   Your kid can walk well on their own.
*   The doctor says your kid needs to walk more.

**13. Is It Safe?**

| Where You Live | Safety Rules |
|---|---|
| US | ASTM F833 |
| Europe | EN 1888 |
| Australia/NZ | AS/NZS 2088 |
| International | ISO 31110 |

Always check for labels that show it has been tested and is safe.

| For Baby Clothes | For Buggies |
|---|---|

| Right size | Easy to move |
| Layers for the weather | Works on different surfaces |
| Easy to wash | Easy to fold and store |

| Good for the environment if possible | Follows safety rules |
